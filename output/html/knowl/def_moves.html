Definition 3.1.8. Reidemeister moves.

Given a knot \(K\) and a diagram \(D\) of \(K\text{,}\) each of the following ways of modifying \(D\) corresponds to an isotopy of \(K\text{.}\)

  1. The type-I move adds or subtracts one crossing.

  2. The type-II move adds or subtracts two crossings.

  3. The type-III move preserves the number of crossings.
