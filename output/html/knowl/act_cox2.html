Activity 1.2.7.

Let \(\sigma\in S_4\) be the permutation that performs the anagram \(\sigma \colon OGRE \mapsto GOER\text{.}\)

  1. Sketch two different braid-a-grams for \(\sigma\text{,}\) using as few crossings as possible.

  2. Express your answer to part (a) as an equation involving the adjacent transpositions \(\sigma_1,\sigma_2,\sigma_3,\sigma_4,\) and/or the trivial permutation \(\epsilon\text{.}\)

  3. Describe how your result might be generalized for anagrams of any number of letters.
