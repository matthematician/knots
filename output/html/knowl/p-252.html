For example, you can check that for the empty tangle \(E\) we have

\begin{equation*} \varphi(E)(x) = x \quad \text{ and }\quad F(E) = \varphi(E)(0) = 0. \end{equation*}

Likewise, for the single twist \(T\) we have

\begin{equation*} \varphi(T)(x) = x + 1\quad \text{ and }\quad F(T) = \varphi(T)(0) = 1. \end{equation*}

Lastly, if we were to rotate a double twist to form \(T^2R\) we would have

\begin{equation*} \varphi(T^2R)(x) = r(t(t(x))) = -\frac{1}{x+2}\quad\text{ and }\quad F(TR^2) = r(t(t(0))) = -\frac12. \end{equation*}

You'll notice that the values of the fraction, labeled as the \(y\)-intercept in FigureÂ 2.2.8, increase by 1 with each twist \(T\) that is added, and are opposite-reciprocated with each rotation \(R\text{.}\)
