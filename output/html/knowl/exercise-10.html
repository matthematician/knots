Exercise 2.

Your co-teacher Giannis is preparing to do the “rational tangle dance” activity with his 8th-grade students. However, in his lesson plan you notice that he's misremembered the tangle function generators as

\begin{equation*} t(x) = x+1 \quad \text{ and }\quad r(x) = \frac{1}{x}. \end{equation*}

Give one reason why his \(r\) function does behave appropriately for the tangle function group. Then, demonstrate using algebra that Giannis' \(t\) and \(r\) do not, in fact, mimic the behavior of the rational tangle group as per Definition 2.2.4.
