Exercise 2.

The order of an element \(\sigma \in S_n\) is the smallest positive integer \(k\) for which

\begin{equation*} \sigma^k := \underbrace{\sigma \circ \sigma \circ \cdots \circ \sigma}_k = \epsilon. \end{equation*}

For example, the transposition \((13)\) has order 2 since \((13)\circ(13) = \epsilon\text{,}\) as the braid-a-gram below shows.

  1. For the three-cycle \(\sigma = (345)\in S_5\text{,}\) sketch a braid-a-gram showing the effect of the permutations \(\sigma, \sigma^2, \sigma^3,\) and \(\sigma^4\) on the word \(BEAST\text{.}\)

  2. What is the order of the permutation \(\sigma = (345)\) according to your answer in (a)? Why?

  3. Following a similar strategy as the above, determine the order of the permutation \(\tau = (13)(245)\) via a braid-a-gram of its powers acting on the word \(LAPSE\text{.}\) (Not all will be English words.)

  4. Predict the order of the permutation \(\kappa = (148)(273956)\text{,}\) based on your previous answers. Explain.
