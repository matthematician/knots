Example 1.1.2.

An example of a unary operation on the set \(S=\mathbb{Z}\) of integers is the negation operation:

\begin{equation*} - \colon \mathbb{Z}\to\mathbb{Z}, \qquad n \mapsto -n. \end{equation*}

Under this operation, for example, \(3 \mapsto -3\) and \(-15 \mapsto 15\text{.}\)

An example of a binary operation on the set \(S = \mathbb{Q}\) of integers is the multiplication operation:

\begin{equation*} \cdot \colon \mathbb{Q}\times\mathbb{Q} \to \mathbb{Q}, \qquad (x,y) \mapsto x\cdot y. \end{equation*}

Under this operation, for example, \((\frac15, -20) \mapsto -4\) and \((-\frac25, -\frac52) \mapsto 1.\)

An example of a ternary operation on the set \(S = \mathbb{R}^3\) of vectors in 3-space is the vector sum:

\begin{equation*} [ -,-,- ] \colon \mathbb{R}^3\times\mathbb{R}^3\times\mathbb{R}^3 \to \mathbb{R}^3, \qquad ({\bf u}, {\bf v},{\bf w}) \mapsto {\bf u} + {\bf v} + {\bf w}. \end{equation*}
