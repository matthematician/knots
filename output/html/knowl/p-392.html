Then \(f\) is a valid \(k\)-coloration if, and only if, at every crossing of arcs \(x_i,x_j,x_k\text{,}\) with \(x_k\) being the overcrossing strand, the following equation holds:

\begin{align} f(x_i) + f(x_j) - 2f(x_k) &=0.\tag{4.1.1} \end{align}

In other words, the color of the over-strand is the average of the colors of the two under-strands.
