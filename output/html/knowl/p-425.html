As Example 4.1.12 suggests, the Takasaki keis \((\mathbb{Z}_k,\triangleright)\) can serve as \(k\)-colorability “detectors” for a knot. When the adjacencies of the arcs in a knot diagram themselves can be mimicked by the operation of \((\mathbb{Z}_k,\triangleright)\text{,}\) then the knot must be \(k\)-colorable. The word “mimicked” here is doing quite a bit of work, however; let's put that idea onto firmer ground.
