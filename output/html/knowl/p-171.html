One way of thinking of a group homomorphism is that it gives us a choice: given two elements in \(G\text{,}\) I can either perform the operation of \(G\) on them first, before applying the function, i.e., calculate \(f(\, x\circ y \,)\text{,}\) or I can apply the function to each element first and then perform the operation of \(H\) on the result, i.e., \(f(x) * f(y)\text{.}\) The two results always agree.
