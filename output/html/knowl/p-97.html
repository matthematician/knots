The identity permutation performs the rather-uninteresting anagram \(BEARS \mapsto BEARS\text{.}\)

  1. Sketch a braid-a-gram for this anagram which includes at least one crossing, but as few as possible.

  2. Express your answer to part (a) as an equation involving the adjacent transpositions \(\sigma_1,\sigma_2,\sigma_3,\sigma_4,\) and/or the trivial permutation \(\epsilon\text{.}\)

  3. Are there other solutions besides the one you found? Describe how your result might be generalized.
