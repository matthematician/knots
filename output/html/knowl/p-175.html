Morally speaking, a homomorphism is a way of comparing two groups. When a homomorphism from \(G\) to \(H\) exists, we might say “some of the algebraic structure in \(H\) resembles the algebraic structure of \(G\text{.}\)” Just how much of the structure in \(H\) resembles \(G\) depends on the range of the function, as well as its ability to tell different elements of its domain apart.
